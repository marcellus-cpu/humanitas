Overview

This Privacy Policy applies to the Humanitas website located at marcellus-cpu.github.io/humanitas ("Humanitas," "we," "our," or "us"). It explains what information we collect when you visit our site, how we use it, who we share it with, and what rights you have.

We take privacy seriously. Humanitas does not sell your personal data. We do not build advertising profiles on individual readers. We collect only what is necessary to operate the site and serve advertising through Google AdSense.

Plain-language summary: We use Google AdSense to display advertisements. AdSense uses cookies to show you relevant ads. We may use Google Analytics to understand how our articles are read. We do not collect your name, email address, or any personally identifiable information unless you voluntarily contact us.

Data we collect

Humanitas collects two categories of information: data you provide voluntarily, and data collected automatically when you visit the site.

Information you provide voluntarily

We may collect your email address if you choose to subscribe to our newsletter. We collect your name, email address, and the content of your message if you contact us by email. We do not collect payment information — Humanitas is free to read.

We do not collect sensitive personal data such as racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious beliefs, health data, or financial information.

Google AdSense

Humanitas uses Google AdSense to display advertisements on this website. Google AdSense is operated by Google LLC, 1600 Amphitheatre Parkway, Mountain View, CA 94043, USA.

How AdSense works

When you visit Humanitas, Google AdSense may set cookies on your device to show you advertisements that are relevant to your interests. These cookies track your browsing behaviour across websites that use Google AdSense. Google may use this information to personalise the ads you see here and on other websites.

What Google collects through AdSense

  • Pages you visit on Humanitas and the time you spend on them
  • Your approximate geographic location (derived from your IP address)
  • Your device type, browser type, and operating system
  • Whether you clicked on an advertisement
  • Your general interests, inferred from your browsing history across Google's advertising network

Google's privacy practices

Humanitas does not have access to the data Google collects through AdSense — this data goes directly to Google and is governed by Google's Privacy Policy. To understand how Google uses data from sites that use its advertising services, see How Google uses information from sites or apps that use our services.

Note on personalised advertising: Google AdSense may serve personalised ads based on your browsing history. You can opt out of personalised advertising by visiting Google's Ad Settings or by visiting aboutads.info.

Humanitas and AdSense revenue

Advertising revenue from Google AdSense is how Humanitas covers its operating costs. We have no editorial relationship with any advertiser — advertisements are served automatically by Google's systems and do not influence the content we publish.

Google Analytics

Humanitas may use Google Analytics to understand how readers interact with our content. Google Analytics is a web analytics service operated by Google LLC.

What Analytics collects

Google Analytics collects information about your visit to Humanitas including the pages you viewed, how long you spent on each page, how you arrived at the site, and your approximate geographic location. This data is used in aggregate — we use it to understand which topics our readers find most valuable, not to identify individual visitors.

IP anonymisation

Where Google Analytics is active on Humanitas, we use IP anonymisation (also called IP masking), which means your IP address is truncated before it is stored by Google. Your full IP address is never stored.

Opting out of Analytics

You can prevent Google Analytics from collecting your data by installing the Google Analytics opt-out browser add-on.

Your rights under GDPR

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you have the following rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and equivalent national laws.

Right of access

You have the right to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.

Right to rectification

You have the right to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data.

Right to erasure

You have the right to request deletion of your personal data ("the right to be forgotten") in certain circumstances.

Right to restrict processing

You have the right to request that we limit how we use your personal data in certain circumstances.

Right to data portability

You have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format.

Right to object

You have the right to object to the processing of your personal data for direct marketing or where we rely on legitimate interests.

Right to withdraw consent

Where processing is based on consent, you have the right to withdraw it at any time without affecting prior processing.

Right to lodge a complaint

You have the right to lodge a complaint with your national data protection authority if you believe we have handled your data unlawfully.

Legal basis for processing

We process data on the following legal bases under GDPR: Consent — for advertising cookies (where required); Legitimate interests — for analytics to improve our content and user experience; Legal obligation — where processing is required by law.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at privacy@humanitas.blog. We will respond within 30 days.

Your rights under CCPA

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA) grants you specific rights regarding your personal information.

Your CCPA rights

  • Right to know: You have the right to request disclosure of the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you, the sources of that information, the business or commercial purpose for collecting it, and the third parties with whom we share it.
  • Right to delete: You have the right to request deletion of personal information we have collected from you, subject to certain exceptions.
  • Right to opt out of sale: You have the right to opt out of the sale of your personal information. Humanitas does not sell personal information.
  • Right to non-discrimination: You have the right not to receive discriminatory treatment for exercising your CCPA rights.
  • Right to correct: You have the right to request correction of inaccurate personal information we maintain about you.
  • Right to limit use of sensitive personal information: You may direct us to limit the use of sensitive personal information. We do not collect sensitive personal information.

Do Not Sell My Personal Information: Humanitas does not sell personal information to third parties. If you wish to exercise any of the above rights, contact us at privacy@humanitas.blog with "CCPA Request" in the subject line.

Children's privacy

Humanitas is intended for a general adult audience. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under the age of 13 (or 16 in the EEA). If you believe we have inadvertently collected information from a child, please contact us at privacy@humanitas.blog and we will delete it promptly.

Data retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes described in this policy or as required by law.

  • Email addresses (newsletter subscribers): retained until you unsubscribe or request deletion.
  • Contact email enquiries: retained for up to 12 months then deleted unless an ongoing correspondence requires otherwise.
  • Analytics data: Google Analytics retains data for 26 months by default. You can request deletion via Google's tools.
  • Advertising cookies: duration varies by cookie — see the cookies table above for specific periods.

Security

Humanitas takes appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against accidental or unlawful loss, alteration, disclosure, or access. These measures include HTTPS encryption for all data in transit, Content Security Policy headers to prevent code injection attacks, and strict security configurations on all pages.

However, no method of transmission over the internet is completely secure. While we strive to protect your data, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, or other factors. When we make changes, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Material changes will be noted in a notice on the Humanitas homepage for a period of 30 days.

We encourage you to review this policy periodically. Your continued use of Humanitas after changes are posted constitutes your acceptance of the revised policy.

How to opt out

You have several options to control data collection and personalised advertising on Humanitas:

Opt out of personalised Google ads

  • Visit Google Ad Settings and turn off ad personalisation
  • Visit the Network Advertising Initiative opt-out page
  • Visit the Digital Advertising Alliance opt-out tool
  • For EU residents: visit Your Online Choices

Opt out of Google Analytics

  • Install the Google Analytics opt-out browser add-on
  • Enable "Do Not Track" in your browser settings (note: not all services honour this signal)

Manage cookies in your browser

You can delete existing cookies and block future cookies through your browser's privacy or security settings. See the cookies section above for browser-specific instructions.

Unsubscribe from the newsletter

Every newsletter email contains an unsubscribe link. You can also request removal by emailing privacy@humanitas.blog.

Contact us

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, wish to exercise your data rights, or have a privacy concern, please contact us. We aim to respond to all privacy enquiries within 30 days.
