About Humanitas

We publish ideas
the world needs to think about

A free, independent publication dedicated to depth over speed, substance over noise, and the belief that great writing still changes how people see the world.

Our mission

Built for people who still believe ideas matter

Humanitas was founded on a single conviction: that the most important things happening in the world are not the ones making the most noise. The rise and fall of civilisations, the laws that govern us, the science reshaping what is possible, the forces driving human behaviour — these deserve rigorous, unhurried attention.

We cover six domains of human knowledge and experience — civilisation, law and technology, the human condition, science, philosophy, and the living world — because understanding any one of them properly requires understanding how they connect to all the others.

Humanitas is free. It always will be. Great ideas should not be paywalled.

The Editorial Philosophy of Humanitas
Editorial values

What we stand for

01

Depth over speed

We do not publish to fill a feed. Every article on Humanitas is written to be read in full, thought about, and returned to. We would rather publish one important piece than ten forgettable ones. If something takes time to understand, we take the time to explain it properly.

02

Ideas over noise

The internet has no shortage of takes, reactions, and hot opinions. Humanitas is interested in something rarer: the insight that changes how you see a problem, the historical parallel that makes the present suddenly legible, the scientific finding that reframes a question you thought was settled.

03

Free. Always.

Knowledge that sits behind a paywall does not circulate. Ideas that cannot be shared do not spread. Humanitas will never charge readers, never require an account, and never compromise its editorial independence for advertising revenue. We are supported by Google AdSense — ads, not subscriptions.

04

Honest about uncertainty

We do not pretend to have answers we do not have. When experts disagree, we say so. When evidence is contested, we show the contest. Intellectual honesty — including the courage to say "we do not know" — is the foundation every other value rests on.

05

Global in scope

Civilisation is not a Western invention. Science is not the property of any one culture. Human experience is not legible through a single lens. Humanitas draws on history, thought, and reporting from every part of the world, because the best ideas about humanity come from all of it.

06

Writing that endures

We measure the success of an article not by how many clicks it received in its first 24 hours, but by whether it is still worth reading in five years. Evergreen depth is our standard. A piece about Roman law should be as valuable to a reader in 2031 as it is today.

The team

A publication built on curiosity

Humanitas is an independent publication. We are not backed by a media conglomerate, a political party, or a venture capital firm. We answer to our readers — and to the ideas themselves.

Our writers include historians, scientists, legal scholars, economists, philosophers, naturalists, and journalists. What they share is a commitment to rigour, clarity, and the belief that every reader deserves to be treated as an intelligent adult.

Write for us

Have something worth saying?

Humanitas publishes writers with deep knowledge of their subject and the ability to communicate it to a curious, non-specialist reader. We welcome pitches from academics, journalists, practitioners, and independent thinkers.

We do not publish press releases, sponsored content, or anything written primarily to promote a product, service, or personal brand. We publish ideas.

Pitch an article

What we look for

A clear argument, deep knowledge of the subject, and prose that respects the reader's intelligence. Long-form preferred — 1,200 to 2,500 words.

Which sections are open

All six sections accept pitches. Strong candidates connect their subject to broader questions about civilisation, science, or the human condition.

How to pitch

Send a 150-word summary of the article, your relevant credentials, and one published writing sample to the address below.

Response time

We aim to respond to all pitches within two weeks. If you have not heard back after three weeks, feel free to follow up once.

Pitch submissions: editor@humanitas.blog
